This demo includes four windows.

The window "Curve" displays the curve X(t), highlights the image of the interval [t0 - h, t0 + h] in red, and shows normal vectors (shown as segments) to this portion of the curve.

The window "Normal Image Vectors" displays the normal vectors shown in the curve window with their tails placed at the origin. The red curve in this window illustrates the change in the normal vector for the highlighted portion of the curve in the "Curve" window.

The window "Graph: kg(t)" displays the graph of kg(t).

The window "Graph: ratio of angle/arclength" displays the angle of the arc shown in hte "Normal Image Vectors" window as a function of t divided by the arclength of the highlighted portion of the curve as a function of t.

Note how the graphs in "Graph: kg(t)" and "Graph: ratio of angle/arclength" compare as h approaches 0.

You can change t0, h, and X(t) in the control window.
