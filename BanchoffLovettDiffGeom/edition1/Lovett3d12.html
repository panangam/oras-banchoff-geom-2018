This demo includes three graphical display windows.

The window "The Exponential Spiral" displays the exponential spiral X(t) = (etcos(t), etsin(t), et), the point X(t0), the position vector X(t0), and the velocity vector X'(t0) with its tail at X(t0).

The window "Position and Velocity Vectors, Normalized" displays the normalized position and velocity vectors X(t0)/|X(t0)| and X'(t0)/|X'(t0)| with their tails at the origin. Note that the angle between these two vectors is constant.

The window "Cosine of angle betewen X(t) and X'(t)" displays the graph of the quantity described in the window title. That the graph has constant height is another way of showing that the angle between the position and velocity vectors is constant.

You can change t0 and X(t) and choose whether or not to show the entire curve in the control window.
