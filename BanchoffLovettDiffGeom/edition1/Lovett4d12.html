The "Curve: X(t)" window in this demonstration displays the curve X(t) = (cos(t), sin(t), cos(2t)/c), the point X(t0), and the tangent, normal, and binormal vectors T(t0), P(t0), and B(t0), with their tails at that point. The "Spherical Images: T, P, B" window displays the same three vectors with their tails at the origin, the sphere on which their heads are constrained to lie, and the binormal indicatrix. The total length of the binormal indicatrix, which is displayed in a readout in the control window, is called the total absolute torsion of X(t). It is possible to change c, t0, and X(t) in the control window. It is also possible to set X(t) equal to circle(t) and spacecardioid(t), listed in the control window.
